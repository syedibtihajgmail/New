Know Your Lookup – Complete Guide

            

                This all‑in‑one network diagnostic suite gives you everything from IP geolocation to email authentication checks and a full speed test. 
                Below is a detailed breakdown of every tool, what it does, and how to get the most out of it.
            


            
🌐 IP Lookup

            
What it does: Finds the geographical location and Internet Service Provider (ISP) of any IPv4 address, or your own public IP.

            
How to use: Enter an IP (e.g., 8.8.8.8) and click Lookup IP, or click Find My IP to auto‑detect your own address.

            
Data shown:

            
    
                
  • IP Address – the queried address.
    • 
                
  • Country – two‑letter country code.
    • 
                
  • City – city name (if available).
    • 
                
  • Region – state/region.
    • 
                
  • ISP – the organisation providing the IP.
    • 
                
  • Registrar – often the same as ISP.
    • 
                
  • Timezone – the time zone of the location.
    • 
            

            
            
🔍 DNS Lookup

            
What it does: Queries any domain for its DNS records. You can choose a specific record type or fetch all common types (A, AAAA, TXT, NS, CNAME).

            
How to use: Enter a domain (e.g., google.com), select the record type from the dropdown, and click Lookup.

            
Record types explained:

            
    
                
  • A – IPv4 address of the domain.
    • 
                
  • AAAA – IPv6 address.
    • 
                
  • MX – Mail Exchange servers (priority and hostname).
    • 
                
  • TXT – Text records (used for SPF, DKIM, DMARC, verification).
    • 
                
  • NS – Name Servers for the domain.
    • 
                
  • CNAME – Canonical name (alias) pointing to another domain.
    • 
                
  • SOA – Start of Authority (administrative info).
    • 
                
  • CAA – Certification Authority Authorization (which CAs can issue certificates).
    • 
                
  • SRV – Service records (hostname and port for specific services).
    • 
                
  • PTR – Pointer record (reverse DNS – maps IP to domain).
    • 
            

            
Additional features:

            
    
                
  • DNSSEC validation – if the domain uses DNSSEC, a green 🔒 DNSSEC validated badge appears.
    • 
                
  • Propagation checker – click 🌐 Check Propagation to compare results from Cloudflare, Google, and Quad9 public resolvers – useful to see if a DNS change has spread.
    • 
            


            
📧 MX Lookup

            
What it does: Specifically retrieves Mail Exchange (MX) records for a domain and resolves each mail server hostname to its IP addresses (both IPv4 and IPv6).

            
How to use: Enter a domain and click Lookup MX.

            
Data shown: A table with Preference (lower = higher priority), Hostname of the mail server, IP Addresses (all resolved A/AAAA records), and TTL.

            
Use case: Troubleshooting email delivery, verifying that your MX records are correctly set up.


            
🛡️ DMARC Suite

            
What it does: Two tools in one: Lookup (check existing DMARC record) and Generator (build a valid DMARC policy).

            
Lookup: Enter a domain and click Lookup DMARC. It searches for a TXT record starting with v=DMARC1 and displays it.

            
Generator: Fill in the fields:

            
    
                
  • Policy (p) – none (monitor), quarantine (mark as spam), or reject (block).
    • 
                
  • Subdomain Policy (sp) – policy for subdomains (optional).
    • 
                
  • Percentage (pct) – percentage of messages to apply the policy to (optional).
    • 
                
  • Aggregate Reports (rua) – email address for aggregate reports.
    • 
                
  • Forensic Reports (ruf) – email for forensic reports.
    • 
                
  • DKIM Alignment (adkim) – r (relaxed) or s (strict).
    • 
                
  • SPF Alignment (aspf) – r (relaxed) or s (strict).
    • 
                
  • Failure Options (fo) – when to send forensic reports.
    • 
                
  • Reporting Format (rf) – usually afrf.
    • 
                
  • Reporting Interval (ri) – seconds between reports.
    • 
            

            
Click Generate DMARC Record to get a ready‑to‑use TXT entry that you can copy and add to your DNS.


            
📨 SPF Suite

            
What it does: Lookup and generate SPF (Sender Policy Framework) records – a TXT record that lists authorised sending IPs for your domain.

            
Lookup: Enter a domain and click Lookup SPF. It finds TXT records starting with v=spf1.

            
Generator: Build your SPF record using an interactive mechanism builder. Add as many mechanisms as needed:

            
    
                
  • Qualifier – + (pass), - (fail), ~ (softfail), ? (neutral).
    • 
                
  • Mechanism type – include, a, mx, ptr, ip4, ip6, exists, redirect, exp, or all.
    • 
                
  • Value – domain, IP, etc. (required for most types).
    • 
            

            
Click Generate SPF Record to get the final TXT entry, which you can copy directly.


            
🔑 DKIM

            
What it does: Looks up DKIM (DomainKeys Identified Mail) public keys stored in DNS. DKIM helps verify that an email was genuinely sent by the claimed domain.

            
How to use: Enter a domain. Optionally provide a custom selector (e.g., mailer). If you leave it blank, the tool tries common selectors: google, s1, s2, k1, default, mail, selector1, 2020.

            
What you see: The raw TXT record containing the DKIM public key (starts with v=DKIM1;) and its TTL.


            
🕵️ IP Leak Test

            
What it does: Reveals your current public IP address and attempts a reverse DNS lookup to find the associated hostname. This is useful to verify if your VPN or proxy is effectively hiding your real IP.

            
How to use: Click Perform Leak Test. The tool will display your public IP, hostname (if a PTR record exists), country, city, and ISP.


            
🔒 Security Checks

            
This tab is split into three main security scanners:


            
IP Security Check

            
Enter an IP address and click Check IP Security. It runs three independent checks:

            
    
                
  • VPN/Proxy/TOR detection – via ipinfo.io’s threat data.
    • 
                
  • Spamhaus Zen blacklist – checks if the IP is listed on the Spamhaus Zen DNSBL.
    • 
            

            
The result is a table with each check’s status, and an overall “safe” or “warning” summary.


            
Domain Security Check

            
Enter a domain and click Check Domain Security. It performs:

            
    
                
  • DNS record presence – checks if A/AAAA, MX, TXT, NS, and CNAME records exist.
    • 
                
  • SSL/TLS certificate analysis – connects to SSL Labs’ API to retrieve the certificate grade, validity date, and issuer.
    • 
            

            
If the SSL analysis is in progress, the tool will retry up to 3 times (2‑second intervals). You can also manually retry using the ↻ Retry SSL button.


            
Domain Blacklist (SURBL)

            
Checks if a domain is listed in the SURBL (Spam URI Realtime Blocklists) – a list of domains that appear in spam messages.

            
How to use: Enter a domain and click Check Blacklist. The tool queries multi.surbl.org via DNS; if the domain is listed, you’ll see a warning.


            
⚡ Speed Test

            
What it does: Measures your internet connection’s ping, download, and upload speeds.

            
How it works:

            
    
                
  • Ping – downloads a 1‑byte file from Cloudflare to measure latency.
    • 
                
  • Download – tries three file sizes: 5 MB (primary), 1 MB, and a jQuery fallback (~30 KB) to ensure a result.
    • 
                
  • Upload – sends a 10 KB payload (and if that fails, 50 KB) to one of three test endpoints (httpbin.org, postman-echo.com, ptsv2.com).
    • 
            

            
Results are shown in Mbps (megabits per second). A detailed log is displayed below the results, showing each step and any fallbacks used.


            
🧰 Additional Website‑Wide Features

            
These tools are available across all tabs:

            
    
                
  • History (📜) – stores your last 20 lookups in your browser’s local storage. Click any history item to re‑run that lookup.
    • 
                
  • Export – every result panel has JSON, CSV, and Copy All buttons to save or share data.
    • 
                
  • Monitoring (🔄 Monitor) – on IP Lookup and DNS Lookup, you can start a 5‑second interval monitor that checks for changes and sends a browser notification if a change is detected.
    • 
                
  • Dark Mode (🌙/☀️) – toggle the entire interface to a dark theme; your preference is saved.
    • 
                
  • Developer Tools (⚙️) – each result panel includes a collapsible dev section showing cache status, response time, and the raw JSON response from the API – useful for debugging.
    • 
                
  • Caching – all lookups are cached for 5 minutes to reduce API calls and speed up repeated queries.
    • 
            


            

                This tool is built with privacy in mind – no data is sent to any server other than the public APIs used (Cloudflare DNS, ipinfo.io, SSL Labs, SURBL). All history and preferences are stored locally in your browser.
